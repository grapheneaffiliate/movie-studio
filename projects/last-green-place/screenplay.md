# THE LAST GREEN PLACE
*50-second wasteland piece — Premise A from brainstorm (fused with B's deadpan)*

## Logline
In a wasteland that worships engines, the most hunted legend is a quiet man
who never fights — because the one thing worth raiding is the garden he simply
refuses to stop watering. **Feeling: defiant peace.**

## Treatment
**Act 1 (0–17 s) — The Hunt.** Pure Fury Road grammar: orange dust, black
plumes, chrome grille, war drums. The war party converges on the rumor every
raider whispers — "the last green place" — and finds a fortress wall of welded
car doors, marked only with a hand-painted green leaf.
**Act 2 (17–35 s) — The Cut.** Hard smash from orange to green. Inside the
wall: a sunlit garden, birdsong, a silver-haired man in a grey t-shirt
watering tomatoes. He feeds his rabbit one lettuce leaf. Outside, the war
party stalls on the ridge. They never attack. They never do.
**Act 3 (35–50 s) — The Button.** He sits, sips tea from an enamel mug, the
orange sky boiling beyond his wall. He lowers welding goggles — to solder a
drip line. Title card. Engines Doppler-fade into birdsong.

## Style page
- **WASTELAND-LOOK** (s01–s04, s08, s11): `gritty 35mm anamorphic film still,
  Mad Max style desert wasteland, burnt-orange dust haze, teal shadow grade,
  blown-out white sun, film grain`
- **GARDEN-LOOK** (s05–s07, s09–s10): `sunlit 35mm film still, warm Kodak
  color, lush green garden bounce light, soft summer morning sun from upper
  left, gentle film grain`
- Lens language: wides 24mm, portraits 50mm, details 85mm macro.
- The orange↔green opposition IS the grade plan; never blend the two looks
  inside one shot except s09 (orange sky above the wall, by design).

## Screenplay

### EXT. OPEN WASTELAND — HARSH NOON

A dead orange plain to the horizon. Three black dust plumes rise, leaning
with speed. War drums.

```
[SPEC s01]  (5 s, WASTELAND-LOOK)
subjects: zero people — empty wasteland only
props:    three tall black dust plumes on the horizon line, cracked orange clay foreground
space:    flat dead plain, low rocky ridge far right, white-hot sky
light:    blown-out white sun overhead, heat shimmer
camera:   24mm wide, locked low to the ground
motion:   dust plumes crawl left-to-right; heat shimmer ripples; foreground static
static:   horizon line, rocks, sky
sound-hook: wind + distant engine swarm + war drums begin (m01, x01)
```

A chrome grille EXPLODES through the dust, all teeth and rivets.

```
[SPEC s02]  (4 s, WASTELAND-LOOK)
subjects: zero people visible — machine only
props:    one rusted chrome truck grille with riveted steel teeth, cracked headlight, barbed-wire wrap
space:    dust cloud fills the entire background
light:    blown-out white sun behind dust, glints on chrome
camera:   85mm, low angle, slight crash push-in
motion:   the truck grille bursts forward through dust toward camera; dust whips past
static:   nothing — full violence of motion (the one all-motion shot)
sound-hook: engine roar peak (x01)
```

## Audio plan
- **m01_war_drums** 0→17 s: percussive war-drum score, taiko + metal hits, accelerating, abrupt stop.
- **m02_garden_theme** 17→50 s: warm pastoral acoustic theme, fingerpicked guitar + soft strings, serene, gentle swell at 38 s.
- **d01_keeper** at ~32 s (over s08): "Not today, lads." — dry, unhurried baritone.
- **x01_engines** 0→17 s: engine swarm, wind, dust hiss. **x02_garden_amb** 17→50 s: birdsong, light leaves, water patter. **x03_goggle_hit** at 42 s: single percussive clack. **x04_doppler** 45→50 s: engine pass fading to silence.
