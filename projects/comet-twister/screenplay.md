# THE SILVER COMET: WINDSHEAR
*50-second superhero piece — the tornado save*

## Logline
A mile-wide tornado bears down on a farm town that has nowhere left to run —
so the Silver Comet flies straight into its throat and spins it apart from the
inside. **Feeling: awe.** The Superman engine isn't beating the storm — it's
the second the wind *stops*, and a town that braced to die simply… doesn't.

## Treatment
**Act 1 (0–16 s) — THE WALL OF WIND.** Golden prairie under a black-green
supercell. A monstrous wedge tornado chews across the fields toward a lone
white farmhouse; a porch swing whips, a screen door tears loose, a child's red
kite is ripped into the dark. Then — a silver streak cuts across the storm sky.
**Act 2 (16–34 s) — INTO THE THROAT.** He arcs and dives straight at the
funnel. Debris — fence planks, a spinning wheelbarrow — orbits him as he bores
in. Inside the column: he plants himself and flies COUNTER to the spin, fists
out, cape streaming the wrong way, the wall of wind shuddering around him.
**Act 3 (34–49 s) — THE STILLNESS.** The funnel unravels into harmless ribbons
of cloud; a single shaft of sun drops onto the farmhouse, untouched. He sets
down in the yard, catches the falling red kite, and hands it back toward a
small distant figure on the porch. Low-angle, cape settling, wheat standing
back up. He lifts off. Sun floods the field. V.O.: "Storm's passed. You're
alright now." Title.
