# THE SILVER COMET
*50-second superhero piece — Superman grammar, original hero*

## Logline
A quiet silver-haired man at a coffee cart hears a cable snap eight blocks
away — and the city learns again why a streak of silver lives in its sky.
**Feeling: uplift.** The Superman engine isn't the punch — it's the moment a
falling thing is suddenly, gently, NOT falling.

## Treatment
**Act 1 (0–16 s) — THE LISTENER.** Golden morning over the city; a silver
streak threads the skyline like a rumor. Below: our man, civilian, paper cup
in hand. His head snaps toward something only he can hear. The glasses come
off — folded with terrible calm. In a brick alley he pulls open his grey
shirt: a silver comet emblem.
**Act 2 (16–32 s) — UP, THEN DOWN.** Launch: ground ring of dust, cape snap,
the rocket climb. Above the clouds, fists forward, sun on his face. Then the
peril: a window-washing rig on a glass tower, one cable whip-snapping, the
platform lurching with one worker aboard. He dives down the glass face.
**Act 3 (32–49 s) — THE CATCH.** He catches the platform underhand — the
fall just… stops. He sets it on the street like a teacup. Low-angle hero
frame, cape settling, papers drifting. A faint smile. Straight up and gone —
a sonic ring and a contrail over the sunset. V.O.: "Just a neighbor, passing
through." Title.

## Character bible
**THE COMET** (the user's likeness — element `keeper-hero`, two refs)
- mid-50s man, **tousled silver-blond hair**, **light stubble**, warm
  half-smile; civilian wears **silver round glasses** + **heather-grey
  crew-neck t-shirt**
- HERO SUIT (original design — no existing-IP marks): **slate-blue fitted
  hero suit**, **polished silver comet emblem (a circle with a swept tail)
  on the chest**, **flowing slate-blue cape with silver lining**, silver belt
- the glasses-off beat IS the transformation; he never wears them in the suit
- voice: warm unhurried baritone — Graham (en); one V.O. line

**THE WORKER** — one distant figure in an orange hi-vis vest on the rig,
never close, face never resolved. Stakes without crowds.

## Style page
- **DAWN-HERO-LOOK** (all): `epic cinematic film still, golden-hour
  superhero blockbuster cinematography, warm amber sunlight with deep blue
  sky, crisp atmospheric haze, anamorphic lens flare, large-format clarity,
  gentle film grain`
- Lens: skies 24mm · hero lows 35mm from below · faces 50mm · inserts 85mm.
- Hero physics rule: when HE moves, the world holds still — the contrast
  sells the power. Cape always moving, even at rest.

## Audio plan
- **m01_wonder** 0→16 s: warm wondrous morning orchestral, gentle strings and glockenspiel, a question rising at the end.
- **m02_soar** 16→49 s: soaring heroic orchestral theme, bold brass fanfare over driving strings — urgent string break mid-way, then warm resolution and one grand final chord.
- **d01_comet** at 45.5 s (Graham): "Just a neighbor, passing through."
- **x01_city** 0→16: morning city, gulls, espresso machine hiss. **x02_launch** at 16.5: deep whump + rocket whoosh + fabric snap. **x03_flight_wind** 20→24: high wind rush. **x04_cable** at 24.3: steel cable SNAP + metal screech. **x05_dive_catch** 28→33: diving wind shriek cut to near-silence then soft metal touch. **x06_boom** at 40.5: rolling sonic boom into distance.

## Rhythm: 4×11 + 5 = 49 s. Hard cuts; fadeblack into title.
