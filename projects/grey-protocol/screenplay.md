# THE GREY PROTOCOL
*50-second action piece — Bourne grammar, one continuous pursuit*

## Logline
A retired asset they should have left alone walks one wet European mile,
burns one phone, and catches one train — because the men hunting him are
fast, but he has been doing this since before they were recruited.
**Feeling: lethal calm.** (Bourne's engine isn't speed — it's a man who is
never surprised.)

## Treatment
**Act 1 (0–16 s) — MADE.** An ops room of surveillance monitors finds his
face. Cut to him already moving: a rain-slick alley, a glance over the
shoulder, a burner phone snapped shut. He knew before they did.
**Act 2 (16–33 s) — THE MILE.** A black SUV takes a wet corner hard. He
answers with geography: rooftop sprint through scattering pigeons, a steel
fire-stair taken three at a time, an empty platform where a train is
already sliding in. The pursuit never touches him — that's the point.
**Act 3 (33–50 s) — THE TRAIN.** Doors close on his coat-tail. The pursuer
arrives one breath late, a black cutout on an emptying platform. Inside:
exhale, city light streaking across his glasses. He looks up into the
carriage's surveillance camera — *he knows we're watching* — and gives one
slow nod. Title.

## Character bible
**THE ASSET** (the user's likeness — element `the-keeper`, from refs/hero_reference.jpg)
- mid-50s man, **short silver-blond side-swept hair**, **round dark-teal
  acetate glasses**, **light grey stubble**; expression: unhurried,
  pre-emptively bored by danger
- wardrobe, never changes: **charcoal waxed field jacket zipped to the chest**
  over **heather-grey crew-neck t-shirt**, **dark jeans**
- movement vocabulary: economical — fast walk, controlled sprint, no panic
- voice: dry, unhurried baritone — Graham (en); one V.O. line only

**THE PURSUER** — one man, never closer than 30 m, **long black raincoat**,
face never resolved (backlit, blurred, or turned). The threat stays abstract;
abstract is scarier and AI-safe.

"matte-black burner flip phone" · "wall of CCTV monitors" · "black SUV with

## Style page
- **BOURNE-LOOK** (all shots): `gritty handheld 35mm film still, cold
  steel-blue and slate-grey grade, desaturated, overcast northern European
  city, rain-slick surfaces, available-light documentary realism, film grain`
- **CCTV-LOOK** (s01, s11 only): `grainy desaturated security-camera frame,
  slight fisheye distortion, scan lines, timestamp-less, cold green-grey cast`
- Lens language: surveillance 14mm fisheye · pursuit wides 28mm handheld ·
  inserts 85mm · faces 50mm. Handheld micro-shake on every pursuit shot.
- Palette discipline: steel blue, slate, sodium-amber accents only (street
  lamps, train interior). No warm daylight anywhere — this film is overcast.

## Audio plan
- **m01_pursuit** 0→33 s: Bourne-style propulsive score — ticking hi-hats, driving string ostinato, electronic sub-bass pulses, brass stabs, relentless acceleration, hard stop.
- **m02_aftermath** 33→50 s: cool-down electronic pulse, sparse piano notes over low strings, controlled and unresolved, two final percussion hits at the end.
- **d01_asset** at ~31 s (over s09): "They picked the wrong city." — dry, unhurried baritone, Graham (en).
- **x01_ops_rain** 0→16 s: electronic monitor chirps fading into city rain ambience. **x02_phone_snap** at 9.5 s: sharp flip-phone snap. **x03_pursuit_bed** 16→28 s: SUV engine surge, tire splash, pigeon wingbeats, boots on steel. **x04_train_doors** 28→33 s: brakes singing, door hiss-thunk, bass-weighted seal. **x05_train_interior** 33→50 s: muffled rail rhythm, gentle carriage sway.

## Shot rhythm check
4+4+4+4+4+5+4+4+4+4+4+5 = 50 s; ~11 one-frame cuts ≈ 0.5 s overlap → ≈49.5 s. All cuts hard (Bourne never dissolves) except fadeblack into s12 title.

### Director's log
- Premise: "the chase that never touches him" — Bourne's grammar (handheld, geography, surveillance paranoia) with the threat kept ≥30 m away at all times (generatability law: no close combat, no crowds; empty-city night staging reads as *more* paranoid, not less).
- Glasses kept as identity anchor; charcoal field jacket added over the grey tee — Bourne palette without losing him.
- Phone screen explicitly out-of-focus (AI text law). Departure boards avoided entirely.
- s11 fisheye nod = the signature shot: he breaks the surveillance fourth wall.
- All cuts hard; the title plate is the only fade. One V.O. line, placed over the pursuer's stillness.
